Home Office FAQ

What does this calculator estimate?
It compares the IRS simplified method with an actual-expense estimate so you can see which approach gives you the larger planning deduction.
Do I have to use the space exclusively for business?
Exclusive and regular use are generally required for the home-office deduction. If the space is also used for personal activities, the deduction may not qualify.
Can renters use this calculator?
Yes. Renters can enter their allocable home expenses and direct office expenses to estimate the deductible portion of a dedicated workspace.
What is included in the simplified method?
The simplified method uses $5 per square foot up to 300 square feet, and direct office expenses can still be considered separately.
Is the deduction limited by business income?
Yes. This estimate caps the deduction at the business income you entered before the home-office deduction, which helps avoid unrealistic outputs.

Compliance Notes

  • This calculator provides planning estimates only and is not tax advice.
  • Exclusive and regular use are important baseline tests for the home-office deduction.
  • Before filing, verify the final deduction rules with IRS guidance or a licensed tax professional.

Refinance Decisions Need Context, Not Just A Lower Rate

This route is dedicated to refinance screening. The calculator helps you compare your current loan with a proposed loan using the numbers that usually decide outcomes in practice: monthly payment delta, breakeven timing, and total lifetime interest impact after closing costs.

Many homeowners only compare APR headline and miss tenure risk. Here we center the decision on expected time in the property and cash-to-close reality, because that is where most avoidable mistakes happen.

How To Interpret The Output

Breakeven month tells you how long monthly savings need to recover upfront costs. If your likely move date is earlier than breakeven, a refinance can still feel attractive but often underperforms in real cash terms.

Three Checks Before You Commit

  • Compare at least two loan estimates from different lenders.
  • Test a conservative move-out timeline, not the optimistic case.
  • Verify final closing disclosure lines against your assumptions.

Mortgage Refinance FAQ

What does breakeven month actually represent?
It is the number of months needed for monthly savings to recover the upfront closing cost. If you sell before that point, the refinance usually does not pay for itself.
Why can my monthly payment drop but lifetime interest still rise?
Extending the loan term can lower monthly payment while increasing total interest paid over time. Always evaluate payment comfort and lifetime cost together.
Should I refinance if rates are only slightly lower?
Sometimes yes, sometimes no. Small rate improvements can still work if balance is large and fees are controlled, but you should test realistic closing-cost assumptions.
Does the calculator include points and prepaid items?
Only if you include them in the closing-cost input. The tool treats closing costs as an upfront cash amount for breakeven and lifetime comparison.
Can I use this for adjustable-rate mortgages?
Not directly. The model assumes fixed rates for both current and new loans, so ARM scenarios need a more detailed projection with future rate paths.

Compliance Notes

  • This calculator provides planning estimates only.
  • For binding loan decisions, confirm terms with your lender and advisor.
  • Rate and fee assumptions should be rechecked before lock.
